Constrained

Minimize within a feasible region — the unconstrained minimum is outside!

Objective
\( \min f(x,y) = (x-2)^2 + (y-2)^2 \quad \text{— min at } (2,2) \text{ (outside!)} \)
Constraint (feasible: inside unit circle)
\( x^2 + y^2 \leq 1 \)
Solution
\( (x^*, y^*) \approx (0.707, 0.707) \quad \text{— on the boundary!} \)

Key Difference

Unconstrained: We can move anywhere. The minimum of \( f(x,y) = x^2 + y^2 \) is at the origin (0,0).
Constrained: Here \( f(x,y) = (x-2)^2 + (y-2)^2 \) has its minimum at (2,2), but that point is outside the unit circle. We must stay inside, so we slide down the "slope" toward (2,2) until we hit the boundary — the constrained minimum is on the circle at \( \approx (0.71, 0.71) \).
